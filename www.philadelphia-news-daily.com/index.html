    Weddings in Philadelphia

    Weddings are always a special occasion as it denotes new life, love, and family. Hence, it should not be taken lightly and every detail of the event should be considered greatly, particularly for the wedding venue. Choosing a wedding venue is very essential and it should not be just any place. It should be a spot where you and your partner feel most comfortable and happy at with the rest of your guests like weddings in Philadelphia.

    Weddings in Philadelphia can never be a hassle-free occasion because it holds some of the best manors, inns, farmhouses, ballrooms, country clubs, estates, hotels, plazas, and practically some other venues that you can think of. Wedding venue in the area is definitely not much of trouble, but taking the necessary preparations to have an organized and successful wedding event is a must. But before getting into the preparations, here are the top wedding venues in Philadelphia to choose from.

    Hyatt Regency Philadelphia at Penn’s Landing – The Hyatt chains of hotels is renowned worldwide and this venue is a topnotch when it comes to intimate weddings, gathering and occasions that are every bit vital in everyone’s lives. It offers elegant furnishings and romantic ambiance that are sure to make every wedding event memorable. It’s also accessible to the Philadelphia airport, boutiques and landmarks that’ll keep you and your partner hooked in the place even after your honeymoon days.

    The Farmhouse at People’s Light and Theatre – If you want a wedding venue that’ll get you closer to nature, this spot is the best choice. It’s an 18th century farmhouse that prides itself of lush environment, breathtaking landscapes and spacious settings that are set to make your wedding occasion ethereal.

    The Manor House at Commonwealth – Set during the 18th century, the Manor House is a place of luxury, fine dining, meticulous service, and superb assistance from the organization and planning up to the day of your wedding. It offers numerous services that’ll keep you in the place from the very first day of your wedding preparations until the very last minute of the ceremony.

    Hotel Sofitel Philadelphia – With 16,000 square foot of coverage, Hotel Sofitel is capable of holding ballrooms, wedding celebrations, galas, and just about any ceremonies that require intimacy, space and beautiful environment. It also offers a wedding reception package that’s already inclusive of the wedding coordinator, dinnerware, bridal suites and complimentary French baths, chilled wine, customized menu cards and many others.

    Talamore Country Club – Isn’t it wonderful to wed in a nearby golf course that is sure enveloped with green surroundings, tranquil environment, lovely amenities and superb service? Talamore Country Club is the ideal spot if you love the idea. It can hold for more than 300+ guests with its 30 ft waterfall and 1,500 sq ft marble dance floor for a whole evening of dancing and merrymaking.

    While selecting the perfect venue is a vital consideration to make, the wedding gown and bridal suits are other essential aspects of the event that should not be neglected as well. These are important as these will make every bride, along with her entourage and mother, look beautiful as ever. Picking the perfect gown can be pretty difficult. You need to consider your body type to highlight your assets and hide your flaws while you walk down the aisle. Therefore, selecting the right bridesmaid dresses should be done months prior to the event. Whether you are planning to have these tailored, rented or bought online from reputable wedding shops like Bridesire, giving ample time for the work to be done on these clothing is crucial. This also goes out to MOB dresses (mother of the bride dresses), in which the right color and right style should be chosen for a matured wearer. With enough time for the tailoring or the alterations of a ready-made gown, you can be confident that these tasks are not done in haste, so perfection is guaranteed. So start choosing the wedding venue today for a mesmerizing wedding ceremony.

    Hurricane ‘Sandy’ to hit Philadelphia on Sunday

     

    A massive hurricane is said to hit the region of Philadelphia, as announced by Gary Szatkowski, the chief meteorologist of National Weather Service in Mount Holly, New Jersey.

    Szatkowski admitted that he was reluctant prior to his terrifying news about the amassing of the tropical storm which they named Sandy.

    As seen by the National Weather Service, Sandy is 330 miles south of Jamaica and is moving almost stealthily with a speed of 5 miles per hour. It is said to hit Philadelphia some time on Sunday.

    The head of the weather bureau even questioned himself upon pushing the button on the alert to the emergency managers of the region, yet he puts his trust in the state-of-the-art facilities and forecast models created by the Europeans and advises the people to prepare for a potentially colossal hit.

    “Sandy looks like a good one,” Szatkowski added.

    The government adds that Hurricane Sandy has a capacity to turn into a “Frankenstorm,” which can greatly shake the entire population corridor of Mid-Atlantic-Northeast with intensive gale, flooding rains, and mammoth waves from Sunday until Holloween.

    “If this pans out, people will be remembering and talking about this a decade from now, “ said Szatkowski.

    Meteorologists also forecast Sandy to interact with a cold front that is crossing the East of the region, potentially creating a devastating “hybrid” which can turn into a tropical beast.

    With lower water level in the rivers and streams, the full impact of Hurricane Sandy might not be visible until Monday or Tuesday, but it already has produced numerous days of terror.

    Rescue teams and organizations such as Red Cross, AAA, Salvation Army, and the emergency managers of the county have heightened their alarm signals. Meteorologists from commercial and private organizations are predicting that Sandy would likely cause at least $1 billion of damage.

    “This is a threat,” claims Szatkowski.
